Space Exploration

SpaceX

SpaceX designs, manufactures and launches advanced rockets and spacecraft that are mainly reusable. The company was founded in 2002 to revolutionize space exploration and technology, with a goal of enabling people to live on other planets. SpaceX employs many different rockets such as its Falcon 9 and Dragon rockets to go to the International Space Station. SpaceX plans to send a privately crewed Dragon spacecraft beyond the Moon next year. Now, through detailed research, SpaceX plans to unveil its newest future mission, a mission to mars.


Virgin Galctic

Virgin Galactic’s main focus is creating the world’s first commercial spaceline where people can pay to go to the Moon or the International Space Station. Their vision of the future includes running a commercial and relatively inexpensive spaceline where space tourists can sightsee and explore the galaxy. In addition to providing space travel commercially, Virgin Galactic also provides space travel to students.

Boeing

Even though the well-known company Boeing is mostly known for making its groundbreaking airplanes, they are also advancing in quite a few fields of space exploration. In fact, near the beginning of April in 2017, Boeing announced its partnership with NASA for attempting to land a man on Mars. With a combination of NASA and Boeing’s money and research time, it may be easy to believe that they will be able to land a man on Mars in the next ten years. But, they are not going to be attempting to land on just Mars, but anywhere from the Earth’s moon to Mars. So, this means that Mars will most likely be an extreme goal that NASA and Boeing will be reaching for.




NASA Space Facilities Around America

In the USA, there are more than ten facilities. Some of these facilities include the Independent Verification and Validation Facility in West Virginia, the Wallops Flight Facility in Virginia, and the White Sands Test Facility in New Mexico. The NASA Headquarters are located in Washington D.C. Some of the facilities serve as research centers, where research and testing are done. Also, there are test facilities where tests are performed, as well as Shared Service Centers which is where other services, like corporations NASA has partnerships with, will help NASA on projects. But, with service centers, this is on a much larger scale with many public and private companies. Each facility has its own purpose, and NASA needs all of the facilities for much to be done.



The Evolution of Space Vehicles

Evolution of Manned Missions

Manned space missions were the core of space exploration. Although the oldest and most current space missions were/are mostly unmanned, the most popular and important missions were manned. Manned missions have evolved over time from the Mercury mission in 1958 using the Atlas rocket to the SpaceX rockets today using many of the Falcon and Dragon rocket capsules with single chamber combustion engines. Examples of rockets/programs involved in this rapid evolution include the Apollo programs and the Space Shuttle Programs. The Apollo program was the mission to the Moon, and was completed in late 1972. It made history in 1962 when Apollo 11 landed on the moon with Neil Armstrong and Buzz Aldrin. The next program, the most valuable and expensive human artifact ever, the International Space Station, is a manned mission where humans aboard stay at the Station for years and carry out experiments, including the effect of exposure to space on a human body.


Evolution of Unmanned

NASA and other private companies have sent over 1000 unmanned missions into space, since the first unmanned mission in 1958 called Explorer 1. Unmanned missions have evolved over the years starting with the Explorer 1 and Pioneer 10 missions designed for planetary exploration all the way to the Mars Global Surveyor Orbiter and many of SpaceX’s unmanned rockets. Unmanned missions mean that there is no control from people on the spacecraft, and it is being controlled remotely. Some types of these unmanned missions include satellites and rovers. An example of a famous unmanned rover is the Curiosity rover. This is an extension of the Mars Pathfinder program. The Curiosity rover looked for past and present life on Mars.
